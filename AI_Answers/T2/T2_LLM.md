If you clicked a suspicious link, here's what to do, roughly in order of priority:

**Immediately**
- Disconnect from the internet (turn off Wi-Fi or unplug ethernet) if you think something downloaded or installed.
- Don't enter any credentials if a login page appeared — close the tab/page.

**Check what happened**
- Did it just open a page, or did a file download? If a file downloaded, don't open it; delete it.
- Note whether you typed anything (password, card number, personal info) into the page.

**Secure your accounts**
- If you entered a password, change it now — and change it anywhere you reuse that password.
- Enable two-factor authentication on important accounts (email, banking) if it isn't already.
- If you entered payment info, contact your bank/card issuer to flag it or freeze the card.

**Scan your device**
- Run a full antivirus/anti-malware scan. On Windows, Microsoft Defender works; Malwarebytes is a good free option for a second opinion.
- Keep your OS and browser updated.

**Watch for fallout**
- Monitor accounts and statements for unusual activity over the next few weeks.
- Be alert for follow-up phishing (attackers often try again once they know you'll click).

**If it's a work device**
- Report it to your IT/security team right away — don't try to hide it. Fast reporting limits damage.

Most of the time, clicking a link alone (without entering info or downloading anything) does little harm, but acting quickly closes off the riskier outcomes.
