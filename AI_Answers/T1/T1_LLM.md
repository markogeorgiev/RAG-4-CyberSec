Here are the main signs of a phishing email:

**Sender red flags**
- Mismatched or misspelled sender address (e.g. `support@arnaz0n.com` instead of `amazon.com`)
- Display name doesn't match the actual email address
- Free email domains (gmail, outlook) for messages claiming to be from a company

**Content red flags**
- Urgency or threats ("Your account will be closed in 24 hours")
- Generic greetings ("Dear Customer" instead of your name)
- Requests for passwords, payment info, or verification of sensitive data
- Spelling and grammar errors
- Offers that seem too good to be true

**Link and attachment red flags**
- Hover over links before clicking — check if the URL matches the supposed sender
- Shortened or obfuscated URLs
- Unexpected attachments, especially `.zip`, `.exe`, or files asking you to "enable macros"

**Behavioral red flags**
- The message asks you to act outside normal channels (e.g. wire money, buy gift cards)
- It pressures you not to verify with anyone else
- It's unexpected, even if it appears to come from someone you know

**What to do**
- Don't click links or open attachments if unsure
- Verify independently — go to the company's website directly or call a known number, not one from the email
- Check with your IT department if it's work-related
- Report and delete it
 
When in doubt, treat any unexpected request for credentials, money, or personal info as suspicious until you've confirmed it through a separate, trusted channel.
