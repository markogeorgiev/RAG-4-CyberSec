Common indicators of malware infection include:

**Performance issues**
- Sudden slowdowns, freezes, or frequent crashes
- High CPU, memory, or disk usage when idle
- Fast battery drain or overheating

**Network activity**
- Unusual outbound traffic or spikes in data usage
- Connections to unfamiliar IP addresses or domains
- DNS settings changed without your action

**Visible/behavioral signs**
- Pop-up ads, especially outside the browser
- Browser homepage, search engine, or new toolbars changed unexpectedly
- Redirects to unwanted websites
- New programs, icons, or files you didn't install
- Disabled antivirus, firewall, or Task Manager / system tools

**Account and access anomalies**
- Passwords changed without your knowledge
- Friends receiving spam from your accounts
- Unfamiliar logins or locked-out accounts

**System-level red flags**
- Files encrypted or renamed with ransom notes (ransomware)
- Missing or corrupted files
- Unexpected reboots or settings changes
- New scheduled tasks, services, or startup entries
- Suspicious processes running under odd names

**Security tool alerts**
- Antivirus detections or quarantine notices
- Repeated security warnings, or being unable to update/run security software

If you suspect an infection, disconnect from the network, run a reputable antivirus/anti-malware scan, change passwords from a clean device, and consider professional help or a clean OS reinstall for serious cases.
